# Part 3: EPAM Systems (Jan 2021 -- Mar 2025)

## 3.1 Role Overview

EPAM Systems is a global technology consultancy. I joined as a Senior Product Manager and worked across three engagements over four years, each for a different client and domain. The thread connecting them: building data-intensive products in regulated or complex environments, progressively moving toward AI-driven solutions.

The consulting model meant working embedded in client organizations -- adapting to their processes, constraints, and stakeholders while bringing product thinking they often lacked internally. Each engagement required fast context acquisition and the ability to influence without direct authority over engineering teams that weren't mine.

## 3.2 AI-Powered Knowledge Management Platform (Feb 2021 -- Oct 2022)

### Context & Problem

The client was building a SaaS workflow suite for engineering and manufacturing companies. The core idea: use AI to extract, organize, and surface project knowledge that typically lives scattered across documents, emails, and people's heads.

Engineering teams lose significant time searching for information from past projects -- design decisions, test results, supplier assessments, compliance records. This platform aimed to make that institutional knowledge searchable and actionable through AI-driven classification and retrieval.

### My Role & Approach

I owned the product roadmap for the AI-driven documentation module. This was my first dedicated AI product role, bridging the gap between data science capabilities and what enterprise customers actually needed.

Key responsibilities:
- **Product roadmap ownership** -- defined what we build, in what order, and why. Balanced AI R&D exploration with features customers were willing to pay for
- **Cross-functional coordination** -- worked across data science, backend engineering, and frontend teams. Translated ML model capabilities into product features with clear acceptance criteria
- **Client-facing demos and feedback loops** -- ran product demos with prospective and existing customers. Used their reactions to validate roadmap priorities and surface gaps between what the AI could do and what users expected
- **Requirements engineering** -- wrote detailed specs for AI-powered features: document ingestion, automated tagging, similarity search, knowledge graph construction

## 3.3 Payments Platform -- European Bank (Oct 2022 -- Mar 2025)

### Context & Problem

The client was a European bank rebuilding its payments infrastructure. The existing system handled basic transfers but couldn't support the bank's growth plans: mobile payments, internal transfers between accounts, and integrations with modern payment rails. The regulated banking environment added complexity -- every feature touched compliance, audit trails, and data privacy requirements.

### My Role & Approach

I was the Senior Product Manager responsible for the payments platform roadmap. This was a shift from AI to fintech, but the core PM work was similar: complex systems, multiple stakeholders, and the need to ship incrementally in an environment where mistakes have real financial consequences.

Key responsibilities:
- **Payments roadmap ownership** -- prioritized across mobile payments, internal transfers, and integration workstreams. Managed dependencies between teams and external payment providers
- **Regulated environment navigation** -- worked within banking compliance frameworks. Features required security reviews, regulatory sign-off, and audit trail design before development could start
- **Stakeholder management** -- coordinated between bank product owners, engineering teams, compliance officers, and third-party payment providers. Aligned competing priorities across business units
- **Customer support tooling** -- identified that operations teams lacked visibility into payment flows, leading to slow issue resolution. Drove the creation of internal tools for payment tracking and troubleshooting

## 3.4 GenAI Consultancy (Mar 2024 -- Mar 2025)

### Context & Problem

While continuing the Payments engagement, I took on a parallel role advising EPAM clients on generative AI adoption. This was during the 2024 wave of enterprise GenAI interest -- organizations knew they wanted to use LLMs but didn't know where to start, what was realistic, or how to avoid expensive dead ends.

### My Role & Approach

I served as a GenAI product consultant, helping clients identify high-value use cases and build proof-of-concept solutions. The work combined technical understanding of LLM capabilities with pragmatic product thinking about what would actually deliver ROI.

Key areas:
- **RAG solution design** -- helped clients build retrieval-augmented generation systems for their internal knowledge bases. This meant evaluating their data landscape, defining retrieval strategies, and setting realistic accuracy expectations
- **Autonomous agent prototyping** -- designed and scoped AI agents that could replace manual verification workflows. Identified where agents could handle routine decisions and where human oversight was still necessary
- **GenAI adoption strategy** -- advised on build vs buy, model selection, cost modeling, and organizational readiness. Helped clients avoid the pattern of building impressive demos that couldn't scale to production
- **Stakeholder education** -- translated AI capabilities and limitations for non-technical executives. Set realistic expectations to prevent the cycle of hype followed by disappointment
